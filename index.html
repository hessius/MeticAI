Setup

How It Works

MeticAI runs on a small computer in your home — like a Raspberry Pi, Mac, or Windows PC — and creates a private web app just for you.

1

Install

Run a one-line installer on Linux/macOS, PowerShell on Windows, or use the macOS app. Everything starts automatically.

2

Open the App

Access MeticAI from any phone, tablet, or computer on your home network. No app store — just your browser.

3

Snap & Brew

Take a photo of your coffee, pick taste tags, and let the AI create a tailored profile uploaded to your machine.

4

Control & Automate

Use the Control Center, schedule preheats, or integrate with Home Assistant and iOS Shortcuts.

Background

Why I Built This

The potential was (and is) basically limitless — my knowledge and time not so.

When I got my Meticulous, after a loooong wait, I was overwhelmed with the options — dialing in was no longer just adjusting grind size, the potential was (and is) basically limitless — my knowledge and time not so.

MeticAI is a growing set of AI tools to enable me, and you, to get the most out of a Meticulous Espresso machine. Among other things it lets you automatically create espresso profiles tailored to your preferences and coffee at hand, understand your espresso profiles and shot data like never before, and ultimately — lets you unleash your Meticulous.

Jesper, coffee nerd and MeticAI creator

Installation

Get Started in 5 Minutes

What You Need

  • A Meticulous Espresso Machine connected to your network
  • A server to run MeticAI (Raspberry Pi, Mac, Linux, or Windows PC)
  • A free Google Gemini API key → Get yours here

Installation

One-line install for Raspberry Pi, Linux, and macOS:

curl -fsSL https://raw.githubusercontent.com/hessius/MeticAI/main/scripts/install.sh | bash

Checks prerequisites, pulls the Docker image, and starts all services automatically.

PowerShell installer for Windows 10/11 (requires Docker Desktop):

irm https://raw.githubusercontent.com/hessius/MeticAI/main/scripts/install.ps1 | iex

Runs in PowerShell 5.1+. See the Windows guide for details.

Prefer a GUI? Download the macOS installer — no terminal required.
